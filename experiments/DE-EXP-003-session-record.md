## 1. Summary Data

The Last Library experiment was the first deliberate attempt to compare human brainstorming performance against DE-assisted LLM brainstorming on the same brief. The researcher completed a 30-minute timed human brainstorm (pen and paper, journal) prior to any LLM sessions to prevent 'ideation contamination' or allow the LLM outputs to influence the human-only testing — see DE-EXP-004.

Five DE sessions followed across 20–24 September 2025, using both Claude (DE v0.49.4c) and ChatGPT (DE v0.49.4). Sessions 1 and 2 were run back-to-back on the evening of Sep 20 (22:35–22:59 UTC) — rapid iteration testing different configurations. Session 3 (commencing 23:30 Sep 20) was a reflective survey session in which the researcher reviewed and compared outputs from all three conditions (human, Claude, ChatGPT). Session 4 (Sep 21 morning) was a further DE run with refined settings. Session 5 (Sep 24) was a deliberate comparison between default ChatGPT and DE-instructed ChatGPT on the same brief.

The anchors used across DE sessions were: **Last / Library / Dystopia**, with a Conceptual column added in the Claude run. The brief was run in both steered and unsteered modes across different sessions.

A repair pass was conducted on the Claude output in Session 3, identifying 47 violations including duplicate stems, overused prefixes ("death-", "final-"), and excessive clustering in the Conceptual column. Full repair pass documented in DE-COMP-001.

## 5. Open Reflection

>Novelty, originiality and surprise become very relevant and explicitly shown in this experiment. What I consider creative, unique or unusual is not the same as what LLMs classify as rare. By setting the dials of the DE to 'rare' or 'abstract' will produce far differnt outputs that 'common' or 'concrete'. As a designer, I must carefully consider the actual goal of the spark generations. Am I directly asking for what Landa refers to as 'attribute listing'? (concrete or conceptual attributes) or am I looking for something that is more detached and ambiguous? The dials are incredibly important and deserves some sustained experimentation with some strict controls/conditions to monitor the deviations.

> In my own process (human), concepts and sparks build upon each other quite quickly. Although I was largely working within the categories, sparks are quite often coming from a chain of associations, with one leading to the next in quick succession requiring very rapid note taking. During the 30 minutes (20 mins + 10 min sessions) there is a need for sustained, focused concentration: getting into the correct divergent flow to facilitate the rapid brainstorming. My own experience, expertise plays an important factor. When compared to the LLM output - the spark creation process is at arm's length and detached and I'm operating a control panel, behind the glass watching from the control room rather than participating in the process itself. 

The human output count was skewed towards Library and Dystopian themes - reflecting my own ability to generate sparks around the topic anchors - however the DE forced the LLM outputs to generate equal outputs for each category. This seems like both a strength and limitation of the DE approach. My own human centered approach allows for wandering and focus to shift whereas the DE/LLM remains locked. For example, my inability to generate sparks on a particular topic may be the result of poor domain knowledge or limitations in my creative skills, vocabulary etc. whereas the LLM has no equivalent limitation in that regard. However, it is also worth considering that perhaps some topics are just much more conceptually rich or offer greater opportunities while others are more limited - and this disparity is then forced onto the LLM which in turn produces the semantic clusters as a result as it seeks variations on a topic with limited avenues of exploration - a variation of the Velvet Problem.

>Claude's output was full of 'violations' that needed the repair pass (47 out of 200). The repair pass process seems like an excellent addition to the DE instructions and it parallels my discussions about how LLMs work - they cannot 'know' what their outputs will be until they are created. The repair pass allows them to analyse their own outputs and correct them. 

>Session 5 was a brief followup investigation, not directly an experimental output of the DE. I was investigating the issue of persistent memory across ChatGPT discussions while using the DE instructions and looking at whether a persistent memory issue would/could influence outputs.
