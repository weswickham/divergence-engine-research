## 4. Practitioner Experience

**At what point did your energy shift? (if it did):**

The sessions ran longer than I had expected, taking much more effort and attention than I had originally anticipated after the long sessions of building the tool. I thought it would be a much cleaner, one-shot process. Entering the parameters for each run, adjusting 'the dials' takes cognitive effort - assessing what has already occured for example and making adjustments to steer future outputs. Engery levels remained high throughout this experiment - partially due to the excitement of having some success with the experiment. (Would this still sustain me in future uses?)

**Did you want to keep going or were you relieved to stop?:**

I was ready to stop at the end - but it was a pragmattic decision - I felt that that I had properly run the Divergence Engine to a logical conclusion by achieving the goal of generating useful sparks and fragments. 

**Anything surprising?:**

At what point does Divergence stop and Convergence begin? Analysis happens almost instantaneously and it is important to remain in the divergent phase - seeking options and possibilities rather than solutions. It is only through my experience (in my own assessment of my skills and expertise) that I am able to remain in the creative/exploratory mode rather than instantly shifting into a more curatorial mode to assess whether these sparks are 'the answer' but instead treating them all as raw fuel and possibilities.

The family predictors is also quite useful - and although the 'over produced' families are largely predictable, the 'under represented' families were very useful in themselves - these could be used to trigger further exploration or be used as the starting points for mind-mapping and branching from them

## 5. Open Reflection

As noted, the issue of divergence and convergence - and the need to keep my frame of mind firmly focused on divergent spark exploration and creative wandering. The long, easy outputs force another mindset - that of curation. Some of it becomes more chore-like - reading long lists, scanning, skimming, searching.
Once the system is 'dialed in' and functioning - the ease of output and the diversity of fragments almost lives up to the AI-hype. However, the developmental and steering burdens are not inconsequential.  
Was naming a dinosaur actually a test of problem-solving - rather than simple fragment generation? I will need to run further tests that are explicitly not seeking a definitive solution. In other words, this test shows the success of the Divergence Engine to produce divergent, exploratory language outputs around specified themes - which is great for brainstorming possible names (eg. for a product, a brand name etc.). It could be argued that I used the DE (in this case) to fulfill the first half of the exploratory, first diamond and I fulfilled the second, convergent function through analysis and selection.
