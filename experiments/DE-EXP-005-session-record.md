## 5. Open Reflection

*Note: This experiment applies the DE to a real creative project — naming an existing body of work — rather than to a design research exercise. This distinction is worth noting in the reflection.*

> As noted above, the LLM is very good at the discussion level — helping to decipher why certain outputs are happening, suggesting modifications and/or specific strategies — and yet the outputs still tend towards the semantic collapse issue, recycling repetitive words and phrases, concepts rather than finding new unique or surprising outputs. In most cases, it feels like I would come up with nearly all of these given enough time — but importantly, I would also discard or disregard particular tracks of thinking (micro-analysis happening at the time of generation), e.g. using the word 'residue' once or twice before relegating it to the discard pile and not using it again, whereas the LLM will continually recycle words. This introduces yet another level of steering burden (stop using the word 'residue') — trying to limit particular words or concepts through caps and forbids.

> It was helpful in helping me define (or redefine) my own brief — forcing me to better articulate what it was I was hoping for. This does not mean I'm presupposing the actual outcome, but it's about being much more explicit about the brief itself and the type of sparks I'm looking for (visual sparks, conceptual sparks, linguistic sparks). This is so very closely related to the design process itself, working with clients, identifying the communication problem and goals. It serves to reinforce that vague inputs will result in suboptimal outputs (GIGO) and that a poorly formulated brief will result in poor conceptual solutions. Will be useful to reconsider this point as I move into other areas of my research.

> Despite all of the controls — the explicit instructions that should control the formatting of the outputs — the variable nature of the LLM response can still cause unexpected issues that seem rudimentary and basic. In this trial, the output format resulted in a single column format rather than a table, and the repair pass was formatted differently. It's not as straightforward and compliant as proper coding instructions — the LLM interprets and takes some liberties as it goes unless there are strict and explicit guardrails and rules laid out. I will have to revisit the instructions and look at tightening up the response format, or at least determine why/how it's diverting from my intentions.
