## Artefact Description

The primary source for this experiment is a handwritten journal kept by the researcher during the experimental period. The relevant pages contain brainstorming outputs produced in two timed sessions using pen and paper, without LLM assistance. These pages serve as the human control condition against which DE outputs in DE-EXP-003 are compared.

Scans of the journal pages are available in the project image archive and are referenced throughout this document. The outputs below are transcribed from those pages via the companion document `The_Last_Library_human_and_AI_compare.docx`, which was prepared by the researcher as a comparative analysis record at the time of the experiment.

**Note on page numbering:** The journal pages are numbered 125 and 59 respectively. These are the researcher's own sequential page numbers within the journal, not experiment identifiers.

## Session Conditions

**Brief:** Brainstorm idea sparks for a book cover for a hypothetical dystopian story titled *The Last Library.*

**Method:** Timed pen-and-paper brainstorm. Researcher worked from the brief without reference to prior LLM outputs. Outputs recorded directly in journal in list/column format.

**Timing:** Researcher used a timer and noted start/end times on the journal page. Session 1 was the primary brainstorm. Session 2 (following morning) added a dedicated Dystopian theme column, noted as "combined" with the Session 1 outputs.

**No LLM involvement:** These sessions were conducted entirely without LLM assistance. Critically, the human brainstorm was completed *before* the equivalent DE sessions in DE-EXP-003 — a deliberate methodological decision to prevent contamination of the researcher's own ideation by prior exposure to LLM outputs. This sequencing establishes the human outputs as a genuine independent baseline rather than a post-hoc comparison.
